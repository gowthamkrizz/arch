Ongoing Excavations

Project Alpha: The Sunken City

Status: Active (Year 3)

Location: Mediterranean Basin

Utilizing deep-sea submersibles and acoustic mapping to reconstruct a coastal trading hub lost to rising sea levels in the late Bronze Age.

Project Beta: Highland Temples

Status: Active (Year 1)

Location: Andes Mountains

Excavating a high-altitude ceremonial complex that challenges our understanding of pre-Columbian climatic adaptation and social hierarchy.

Completed Projects

The Desert Tombs (2020-2024)

Uncovered a necropolis containing intact sarcophagi and extensive hieroglyphic texts detailing the transition between two dynasties.

Valley of Kings Survey (2018-2021)

Comprehensive LIDAR scan of the greater valley area, resulting in the identification of 12 new potential tomb entrances.

Celtic Hillforts (2015-2018)

Excavation of a massive Iron Age defensive structure, revealing intricate metalworking workshops and trade goods from across Europe.

Site Mapping & Surveys

Before any physical excavation begins, Stackly employs state-of-the-art surveying techniques. This minimizes ecological impact and ensures we only excavate where necessary.

  • Satellite Photogrammetry
  • Aerial LIDAR Scans
  • Ground-Penetrating Radar (GPR)

Standard Project Timeline

Phase 1: Survey & Permitting

Months 1-6. Non-invasive mapping and securing governmental and ethical approvals.

Phase 2: Initial Excavation

Months 7-18. Breaking ground, establishing stratigraphy, and careful artifact recovery.

Phase 3: Analysis

Months 19-30. Lab work, carbon dating, isotopic analysis, and 3D digital preservation.

Phase 4: Publication

Months 31+. Peer-reviewed publishing and museum exhibition coordination.
